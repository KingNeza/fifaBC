Vancouver, Canada

Vision

To establish Latin House FIFA 2026 as the premier celebration hub in Vancouver for Latin American football culture, creating an unforgettable experience that unites communities, celebrates athletic excellence, and strengthens cultural bonds between Latin America and Canada during the world's greatest sporting event.

Mission

To provide an electrifying and inclusive platform for Latin American communities, football fans, and cultural enthusiasts to gather, celebrate, and experience the passion of FIFA 2026. Latin House will serve as the beating heart of Latin American football culture in Vancouver, offering authentic experiences, sports activities, cultural celebrations, and community engagement that honor the rich football heritage of participating nations.

About

Latin House FIFA 2026 will be a dynamic celebration hub located in Vancouver during the FIFA World Cup 2026. This innovative space will serve as the official gathering place for Latin American football culture, designed to bring together fans from across the Americas and beyond to celebrate the beautiful game. With Vancouver hosting multiple World Cup matches, Latin House will be the epicenter of Latin passion, culture, and sporting excellence.

Background

The FIFA World Cup 2026 marks a historic moment as it comes to North America, with Canada co-hosting alongside Mexico and the United States. Vancouver will welcome the world's best football and their passionate supporters. This presents an unprecedented opportunity to showcase Latin American football culture, passion, and community spirit in one of Canada's most vibrant and diverse cities.

Latin America has a storied football heritage that captivates billions worldwide. With multiple Latin American nations competing in the tournament, there is a vital need for a dedicated space where these communities can unite, celebrate their teams, and share their rich football traditions with Canadian hosts and international visitors.

Games & Tournaments

Match Day Viewing Parties

Giant screens broadcasting all Latin American team matches with authentic stadium atmosphere, live commentary in Spanish and Portuguese, and thousands of passionate fans creating an unforgettable experience.

Soccer Tournaments

Latincouver will host tournaments for adults and kids, celebrating the passion of fútbol across generations. The Kids Tournament (ages 3–12) will engage 80 young players in training sessions, team formation, and matches leading up to semi-finals and finals on the festival's main field, complete with medals and certificates. The Adult Tournament will bring high-energy competition, community pride, and the thrill of championship play.

Funding Resources

  • FIFA & Football Associations: Partnership opportunities with FIFA, CONMEBOL, and national football federations from participating countries for official World Cup activation support.
  • Private Sponsors & Corporate Partners: Major sports brands (Adidas, Nike, Puma), beverage companies, telecommunications providers, and financial institutions interested in reaching passionate football audiences.
  • Latin American Business Community: Engaging Latin American chambers of commerce in Vancouver, Canadian importers of Latin American products, and businesses serving Latin American communities.
  • Government of Canada: Securing funding from Canadian Heritage for multicultural programming, Tourism Vancouver for visitor experiences, and federal departments supporting FIFA 2026 legacy projects.
  • Consulates & Embassies: Partnerships with Latin American diplomatic missions in Canada for cultural programming, official team support, and community engagement initiatives.
  • Ticket Sales & Merchandise: Revenue from ticketed events, official Latin House merchandise, team jerseys and scarves, and commemorative World Cup items.
  • Food & Beverage Operations: Revenue from authentic Latin American food vendors, beverage sales, and premium hospitality packages for corporate and VIP guests.

Business Model

Tiered Fan Membership Program: Offering season passes with exclusive benefits including priority seating at viewing parties, merchandise discounts, and access to meet-and-greet events with football legends.

Premium Hospitality Packages: VIP experiences with premium seating, all-inclusive food and beverage, exclusive areas, and special access to events and activities.

Corporate Sponsorships: Presenting sponsors for major events, branded activation zones, stadium signage opportunities, and official supplier partnerships.

Event & Space Rental: Corporate events, private viewing parties, tournament hosting, and brand activation space for companies targeting football demographics.

Merchandise Sales: Official Latin House FIFA 2026 branded items, team merchandise, commemorative products, and exclusive limited-edition collections.

Food & Beverage Revenue: Multiple food stations featuring authentic cuisine from participating nations, full bar service, and premium catering options.

Timeline

Phase 1: Planning & Preparation (November 2025 - February 2026)

Secure venue location in Vancouver, finalize partnerships with FIFA and football associations, launch fundraising campaigns, recruit key personnel including operations director and programming manager, develop comprehensive marketing strategy, and establish relationships with Latin American community organizations.

Phase 2: Build-Out & Launch (March - May 2026)

Complete venue transformation with giant screens and football-themed design, install technical infrastructure for broadcasts and connectivity, finalize programming schedule aligned with World Cup match dates, launch ticket sales and membership program, secure food and beverage vendors, and execute pre-tournament marketing blitz.

Phase 3: Grand Opening & Tournament Operations (June 11 - July 19, 2026)

Official grand opening celebration with former football legends, daily operations during the entire World Cup tournament, match day viewing parties for all Latin American team games, cultural programming and tournaments, fan engagement activities, and real-time social media coverage creating viral moments.

Phase 4: Legacy & Impact Assessment (August - December 2026)

Comprehensive evaluation of economic and cultural impact, documentation of community connections made, assessment of commercial success, planning for future sports and cultural events, establishing Latin House as a model for future international sporting events, and exploring permanent cultural center opportunities.
